Compare association measures

Correlation coefficients quantify the association between two variables, but not all coefficients capture the same thing. Pearson r measures linear association and is sensitive to outliers; Spearman ρ and Kendall τ use ranks and detect monotonic relationships; mutual information and distance correlation pick up arbitrary nonlinear dependencies.

At what sample size do correlations stabilize?

Sample correlations converge to the population value as N grows, but estimates in small samples can be wildly inaccurate. A correlation that starts at r = .69 with 20 participants might settle at r = .26 by the time you have 274. The corridor of stability (COS) is a band of width ±w around the true ρ, and the point of stability (POS) is the sample size from which the running correlation never again leaves this corridor (Schönbrodt & Perugini, 2013).
