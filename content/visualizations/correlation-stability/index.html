At What Sample Size Do Correlations Stabilize?

Interactive simulation — based on Schönbrodt & Perugini (2013)
Background

Correlation trajectories

Sample correlations converge to the population value as N grows, but estimates in small samples can be wildly inaccurate. A correlation that starts at r = .69 with 20 participants might settle at r = .26 by the time you have 274.

The corridor of stability (COS) is a band of width ±w around the true ρ. The point of stability (POS) is the sample size from which the running correlation never again leaves this corridor. Below, you can simulate this process and see how many participants you really need.

Bottom line: For typical effect sizes in personality psychology (r ≈ .21), with a corridor of ±.10 and 80% confidence, you need roughly N ≈ 250 for a stable correlation estimate. There are few scenarios where going below N = 150 is justifiable.
