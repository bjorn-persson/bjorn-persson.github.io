Cronbach's Alpha and Measurement Reliability

How internal consistency determines measurement precision and the width of confidence intervals around observed scores

Cronbach's α determines the Standard Error of Measurement (SEM): how much a single observed score is expected to differ from a person's true score. A score is not a point — it is an interval. Low α widens that interval and increases classification errors.

SEM = SD × √(1 − α). The 95% true-score CI for an observed score X is [X − 1.96·SEM, X + 1.96·SEM]. Rules of thumb: α ≥ 0.90 for high-stakes individual decisions; α ≥ 0.70 for research/group comparisons.
