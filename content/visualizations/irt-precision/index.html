IRT Precision

In IRT, measurement precision is not constant — it depends on where a person falls on the latent trait. The Test Information Function I(θ) determines the local Standard Error: SE(θ) = 1/√I(θ). A test can be highly reliable at one trait level and nearly useless at another.

Test information function

I(θ) = ∑ ai² Pi(θ) Qi(θ) (2PL).   SE(θ) = 1 / √I(θ).   Local reliability ρ(θ) = 1 − 1/I(θ) when σ² = 1.
Unlike Cronbach's α, IRT precision varies across the trait continuum: a test is most precise where information peaks.
